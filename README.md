# D2C Customer Churn Intelligence – Part 2

## Objective

The objective of this project is to create actionable customer segments using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis combined with behavioral and support signals. The goal is to identify which customers should be prioritized for retention before deploying a machine learning model.

---

## Dataset Used

The following datasets were used:

* customers.csv
* orders.csv
* support_tickets.csv
* web_events_snapshot.csv
* intervention_history.csv
* churn_labels.csv

Only data available on or before the snapshot date was used for feature engineering.

---

## RFM Methodology

### Recency

Number of days since the customer's most recent purchase before the snapshot date.

### Frequency

Total number of purchases made before the snapshot date.

### Monetary

Total revenue generated by the customer before the snapshot date.

---

## Additional Behavioral Signals

To improve segmentation, RFM was combined with:

1. Support Ticket Count
2. Return Rate
3. Website Sessions
4. Campaign Engagement
5. Manual Priority Bucket

---

## Customer Segments

### Champions

Recent, frequent, and high-value customers.

### Loyal Customers

Frequent customers with consistent purchasing behavior.

### High Value Unhappy

High-spending customers with multiple complaints or support issues.

### At Risk

Previously active customers showing significant inactivity.

### Discount Sensitive

Customers with elevated return rates and promotion dependency.

### Dormant

Low-engagement customers with infrequent purchases.

## Retention Strategy

### Champions

Retention Action:
VIP programs and early product launches.

### Loyal Customers

Retention Action:
Loyalty rewards and referral incentives.

### High Value Unhappy

Retention Action:
Dedicated customer success outreach.

### At Risk

Retention Action:
Win-back campaigns and personalized recommendations.

### Discount Sensitive

Retention Action:
Bundle offers instead of blanket discounts.

### Dormant

Retention Action:
Low-cost automated reactivation campaigns.
