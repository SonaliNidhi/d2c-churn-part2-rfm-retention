# Campaign Budget Prioritization

## Total Retention Budget

₹50,000

The retention budget is allocated based on two factors:

1. Churn Risk
2. Expected Business Value

Segments with both high churn probability and high revenue contribution receive the highest investment priority.

| Priority | Segment            | Budget Allocation (₹) | Justification                                                                                                    |
| -------- | ------------------ | --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | High Value Unhappy | 20,000                | High-value customers with dissatisfaction signals. Losing these customers would have the largest revenue impact. |
| 2        | At Risk            | 15,000                | Customers showing inactivity but with historical purchasing behavior. Strong opportunity for recovery.           |
| 3        | Loyal Customers    | 8,000                 | Existing repeat customers who can be retained through loyalty incentives and relationship building.              |
| 4        | Champions          | 4,000                 | Already highly engaged customers requiring low-cost retention initiatives.                                       |
| 5        | Discount Sensitive | 2,000                 | Customers responsive to promotions but with moderate long-term value.                                            |
| 6        | Dormant            | 1,000                 | Lowest expected ROI. Focus on automated, low-cost reactivation campaigns.                                        |

## Budget Allocation Strategy

The company should prioritize retention spending where the expected revenue preservation is highest.

High Value Unhappy and At Risk customers represent the strongest combination of revenue impact and churn risk and therefore account for 70% of the total retention budget.

## Expected Business Impact

* Reduce churn among high-value customers.
* Improve customer lifetime value.
* Increase campaign efficiency.
* Avoid unnecessary blanket discounting.
* Improve return on retention spend.
