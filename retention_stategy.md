# Retention Strategy

## Executive Summary

The objective of this strategy is to maximize customer retention while operating under a limited retention budget of ₹50,000. Customer segments were created using RFM analysis combined with support-ticket activity, return behavior, campaign engagement, and web activity.

Priority is given to customers who have both high churn risk and high business value.

---

# Segment 1: Champions

## Customer Behaviour

* Recent purchasers
* High order frequency
* High revenue contribution
* Strong website engagement

## Churn Risk

Low

## Business Value

Very High

## Retention Action

* VIP loyalty program
* Early access to new products
* Exclusive product launches

## Campaign Type

Relationship-building campaign

## Budget Allocation

₹4,000

## Success KPI

* Repeat purchase rate
* Loyalty program participation

---

# Segment 2: Loyal Customers

## Customer Behaviour

* Consistent purchasing pattern
* Moderate-to-high revenue contribution
* Active engagement

## Churn Risk

Medium

## Business Value

High

## Retention Action

* Loyalty rewards
* Membership upgrades
* Referral incentives

## Campaign Type

Retention and upsell campaign

## Budget Allocation

₹8,000

## Expected Business Impact

Increase customer lifetime value and reduce migration to competitors.

---

# Segment 3: High Value Unhappy

## Customer Behaviour

* High revenue contribution
* Multiple support complaints
* High dissatisfaction signals

## Churn Risk

Very High

## Business Value

Very High

## Retention Action

* Dedicated customer-success outreach
* Personalized resolution process
* Priority support queue

## Campaign Type

Service recovery campaign

## Budget Allocation

₹20,000

## Why Prioritized First

These customers contribute significant revenue and are at high risk of leaving.

## Expected Business Impact

Highest ROI retention opportunity.

---

# Segment 4: At Risk

## Customer Behaviour

* Previously active
* Long period of inactivity
* Reduced engagement

## Churn Risk

High

## Business Value

Medium to High

## Retention Action

* Win-back campaign
* Personalized recommendations
* Free shipping incentives

## Campaign Type

Reactivation campaign

## Budget Allocation

₹15,000

## Expected Business Impact

Recover dormant revenue streams.

---

# Segment 5: Discount Sensitive

## Customer Behaviour

* High return rate
* Strong promotion dependency
* Lower brand loyalty

## Churn Risk

Medium

## Business Value

Medium

## Retention Action

* Product bundles
* Personalized offers
* Limited-time incentives

## Campaign Type

Promotion optimization campaign

## Budget Allocation

₹2,000

## Expected Business Impact

Reduce unnecessary discount spending.

---

# Segment 6: Dormant

## Customer Behaviour

* Low engagement
* Low purchase frequency
* Low recent activity

## Churn Risk

High

## Business Value

Low

## Retention Action

* Automated email journeys
* Low-cost reactivation campaigns

## Campaign Type

Automated nurture campaign

## Budget Allocation

₹1,000

## Expected Business Impact

Low-cost opportunity to reactivate inactive customers.

---

# Budget Prioritization

| Segment            | Budget (₹) | Priority |
| ------------------ | ---------- | -------- |
| High Value Unhappy | 20,000     | 1        |
| At Risk            | 15,000     | 2        |
| Loyal Customers    | 8,000      | 3        |
| Champions          | 4,000      | 4        |
| Discount Sensitive | 2,000      | 5        |
| Dormant            | 1,000      | 6        |

---

# Strategic Recommendation

The company should prioritize High Value Unhappy and At Risk customers first because they combine high churn probability with significant revenue impact. Champions and Loyal Customers should receive relationship-building campaigns rather than costly discount programs.
